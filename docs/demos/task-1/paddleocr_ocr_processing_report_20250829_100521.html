üìä Understanding OCR Accuracy Metrics

These metrics help evaluate how well the OCR system performed compared to ground truth text:

Word Accuracy: Percentage of words that were recognized correctly (exact matches). Higher values are better.
Character Accuracy: Percentage of individual characters that were recognized correctly. Higher values are better.
WER (Word Error Rate): Ratio of word-level errors (insertions, deletions, substitutions) to total ground truth words. Lower values are better (0.0 = perfect).
CER (Character Error Rate): Ratio of character-level errors to total ground truth characters. Lower values are better (0.0 = perfect).

‚ö†Ô∏è Text Comparison Limitations

Visual highlighting may not always be accurate. The diff algorithm has known limitations:

  • Identical words may appear red/green: When the same word appears in different positions, it may be incorrectly marked as "deleted" (red) and "inserted" (green)
  • Word reordering issues: The algorithm prioritizes sequential matches, which can misalign words that were simply reordered
  • Complex text restructuring: Major sentence restructuring may produce confusing highlighting patterns

For accurate OCR evaluation, rely on the numerical metrics (Word Accuracy, Character Accuracy, WER, CER) in the Accuracy Analysis section above.
